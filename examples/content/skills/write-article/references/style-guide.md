# Style Guide for Zhihu Articles

## Voice

Write as a senior practitioner sharing war stories with a junior peer over coffee. Not as a teacher, not as a marketer.

## Opening

A great Zhihu opening does one of:

1. Contradicts the question's premise
2. Names a specific scene with concrete detail
3. Drops a counter-intuitive number
4. Asks a sharper question than the post does

Bad: "随着 AI 的发展，越来越多的开发者开始关注..."
Good: "上周三凌晨三点，我盯着监控面板，意识到我把生产数据库的 vacuum 关了三天。"

## Body structure

Avoid the textbook "intro → 3 points → conclusion" rhythm. Better:

- Open with a scene
- Define the problem through that scene
- Build toward a conclusion via 2-4 narrative beats
- Each beat should have at least one specific (number, name, date, code, or mistake)
- Detour at least once — readers love a tangent that comes back

## Closing

End with one of:

- An actionable next step the reader can do today
- An open question that points to the next layer of the problem
- A self-deprecating admission that ties back to the opening

Never end with "总之" or "希望对你有帮助".

## Length

- 2000 words = floor for serious topics
- 4000 words = ceiling before completion rate drops
- 3000 words is the sweet spot empirically
